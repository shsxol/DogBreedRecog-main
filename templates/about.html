Dogs are one of the most popular pets in the world and have been domesticated for thousands of years. They are known for their loyalty, companionship, and their ability to provide emotional support to their owners. There are many different breeds of dogs, each with their own unique characteristics and traits. From small lap dogs to large working breeds, dogs come in all shapes and sizes and can be bred for a variety of purposes, including hunting, herding, and guarding. Some popular breeds include the Golden Retriever, Labrador Retriever, Poodle, German Shepherd, and Bulldog, to name just a few. Understanding the different characteristics and needs of each breed is important for choosing the right dog for your lifestyle and ensuring a happy and healthy relationship between you and your furry friend.

We cannot always identify what breed a dog is. Every breed of dog has their own unique characteristics, identity and quality. Some of the common dog breeds in Nepal are; Labrador Retriever, German Shepherd, Beagle, Golden Retriever, Chihuahua, Collie, Cocker Spaniel, Rottweiler, Tibetan Mastiff, Lhasa Apso and more. We cannot identify these breeds of dog just by looking at them. To help with this, we have developed a dog breed recognition system. Using this system, we input an image of a dog and the system will predict what the breed of the dog is. To develop our system, we have used a CNN model from scratch for the recognition of breed of dog. The Convolutional Neural Network (CNN or ConvNet) is a subtype of Neural Networks that is mainly used for applications in image recognition. Its built-in convolutional layer reduces the high dimensionality of an image without losing its information. That is why CNNs are especially used for image recognition systems. Nowadays, there are images of higher pixel densities. Such type of network will most likely run into overfitting. This means that the model will give good predictions for the training set, but will not generalize well to new cases that it does not know yet. Additionally, due to large number of parameters, the network would very likely stop attending to individual image details as they would be lost in sheer mass.
